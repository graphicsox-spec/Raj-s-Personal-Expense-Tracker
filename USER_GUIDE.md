# Raj's Personal & Family Expense Tracker - User Guide

Welcome to the Personal & Family Expense Tracker! This application is designed to give you a complete, holistic view of your family's finances. It is built to be simple to use while offering detailed tracking across various financial categories.

---

## 📌 1. Dashboard Overview
The **Dashboard** is your central financial hub.
- **Set Monthly Income**: Click the "Set Income" button to input your total expected family income for the month. This value is saved locally in your browser.
- **Top Metrics**: Instantly view your total income, this month's total variable spending, your current savings, and the sum of all your outstanding credit card balances.
- **Fixed Costs**: See exactly how much you are spending on non-negotiable costs like your Mortgage and Utilities.
- **Visual Charts**: The Pie chart breaks down your variable spending by category, making it easy to spot where your money is going. The Bar chart shows your spending trend over the last 6 months.

---

## 🧾 2. Expenses (Daily Tracking)
The **Expenses** tab is for your day-to-day variable spending.
- **What to track here**: Groceries, Dining Out, Gas, Entertainment, Personal Care, and miscellaneous daily purchases.
- **How to use**: Simply select the date, category, amount, and payment mode. It will instantly update your Dashboard charts and Remaining Budget.

---

## 🐷 3. Budget (Monthly Limits)
The **Budget** tab allows you to set spending goals for specific categories.
- **What to track here**: Set a $800 limit for Groceries or a $200 limit for Dining Out.
- **How it works**: The app calculates your total allocated budget and subtracts your actual spending (from the Expenses tab) to show your "Remaining Budget" on the Dashboard.

---

## 📅 4. Recurring Bills
Never miss a payment with the **Recurring Bills** tab.
- **What to track here**: Fixed monthly costs like Electricity, Water, Internet, Phone bills, and Subscriptions.
- **How to use**: Enter the bill name, the day of the month it's due, the expected amount, and whether Auto Pay is enabled. This helps you ensure enough funds are in your checking account before the due date.

---

## 💳 5. Credit Cards
The **Credit Cards** tab gives you a bird's-eye view of your debt and payment schedules.
- **What to track here**: Chase Sapphire, Amex, Capital One, etc.
- **How to use**: Log your current statement balance, the minimum due, and the due date. Mark the "Paid?" column as 'Yes' once you've scheduled your payment for the month.

---

## 🏠 6. Home Expenses & 📆 Annual Expenses
These tabs are designed for long-term or infrequent project costs.
- **Home Expenses**: Use this for tracking home renovations, furniture purchases, or major repairs (e.g., "Kitchen Remodel", "New HVAC").
- **Annual Expenses**: Use this for bills that only happen once or twice a year, like Property Taxes, Vehicle Registration renewals, or Annual Insurance premiums.

---

## 💾 How Your Data is Saved
This application uses **Google Sheets** as its database backend. 
When you add, edit, or delete a record, it instantly syncs with your personal Google Sheet, ensuring your data is always safe, private, and accessible.

*(Note: If the app is disconnected from Google Sheets during local testing, it uses "Mock Data" stored in your browser's local storage so you can continue testing features uninterrupted.)*
